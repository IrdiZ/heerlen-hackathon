# Albanian Citizens Immigrating to the Netherlands: Real Experiences

*Research compiled: February 2026*

## Overview: Albanian Community in NL

According to various sources, there are between 12,000-60,000 ethnic Albanians living in the Netherlands:
- Official statistics: ~12,000 people from Albania proper
- Kosovo-Albanian Diaspora Ministry estimates: ~60,000 ethnic Albanians (including Kosovo Albanians)
- Many emigrated from Kosovo and North Macedonia
- Major cities: Amsterdam, Rotterdam, Utrecht
- ~20,000 Kosovo Albanians emigrated since the 1999 Kosovo War

The Netherlands Diaspora Ministry considers NL to have "one of the best conditions for the Albanian diaspora."

### 1. MVV Applications: You CANNOT Apply in Albania!

**This catches many Albanians off guard.** The Dutch Embassy in Tirana does NOT process MVV (long-stay visa) applications.

**Where Albanians must go:**
- **Embassy in Skopje, North Macedonia** for MVV applications
- Travel to Skopje required (additional cost, 5+ hours journey)
- Appointment must be booked online via VFS
- After appointment, passport collection can be done in Tirana OR Skopje

**Documents cannot be picked up same day** â€” processing takes up to 10 working days.

### 2. Document Legalization Requirements

Albanian documents require a **two-step process**:

**Step 1: Translation**
- Documents in Albanian MUST be translated into Dutch, English, French, or German
- Must use a sworn translator

**Step 2: Apostille**
- Get apostille from Albanian Ministry of Foreign Affairs
- Both document AND translation need apostille
- Contact: HCCH website for Albanian MFA details

**âš ï¸ Important Warning:**
> "Legalisation does not prove the authenticity of a document or the truthfulness of its content. A municipality in the Netherlands, the Immigration and Naturalisation Service (IND) or another authority may decide to verify these things."

**Common Document Issues:**
- Birth certificates often need extra verification
- Marriage certificates may require additional proof
- Civil status documents frequently questioned
- Old documents from communist era can be problematic
- Name spelling variations between Albanian documents causes delays

### 3. Civic Integration Exam Abroad (Basisexamen)

Albanians must pass this exam BEFORE applying for certain visas (family reunification, partner visa):
- Tests Dutch language basics (A1 level)
- Tests knowledge of Dutch society
- Exam taken at Dutch Embassy in Skopje
- Valid for 1 year
- Cost: ~â‚¬150

**Exemptions:**
- Under 18 or over state pension age
- Already have Dutch education diplomas
- Coming for temporary purposes (work, study)
- Family of asylum permit holder

## ðŸ  Practical Challenges for Albanians

### Housing Crisis

The Netherlands has a severe housing shortage. Challenges specific to immigrants:

1. **Registration requirement**: You need an address to register (BSN), but landlords want BSN to rent
2. **Illegal subletting common**: Some landlords won't allow registration (red flag!)
3. **Anti-immigration sentiment**: Some landlords explicitly prefer "Dutch only"
4. **High deposits**: Often 2-3 months rent upfront + first month

> "Some unscrupulous landlords do this to avoid having to pay higher municipal taxes... It is unwise to rent a place where you cannot register as it can cause quite a bit of trouble down the road."

**Important Rule (from January 2026):**
> "Only non-residents with a European passport can register in the RNI at the municipality of The Hague."

This means Albanians now have additional hurdles for non-resident registration.

### Job Market Discrimination

Studies show significant discrimination in the Dutch labor market:

- 55% of people in NL face discrimination when applying for jobs
- 27% experienced discrimination based on origin/ethnicity
- Non-Western migrants face higher discrimination rates
- Name-based discrimination documented in studies
- Networking disadvantages for newcomers

> "Migrants experience more discrimination than native Dutch, mostly in the form of hurtful jokes."
> â€” Emerald Research Study

### Language Barrier

English is widely spoken, BUT:
- Many official documents only in Dutch
- Integration exam requires Dutch (A2 level minimum)
- Better jobs require Dutch fluency
- Social integration harder without Dutch

## âœ… Step-by-Step: Albanian Immigrating to NL

### For Partner/Family Reunification:

1. **Prepare documents in Albania**
   - Get birth certificate, marriage certificate
   - Have translated by sworn translator
   - Get apostille from Albanian MFA
   - Allow 2-4 weeks for this process

2. **Pass Basic Integration Exam** (if applicable)
   - Study Dutch A1 level
   - Learn about Dutch society
   - Book exam at Skopje embassy
   
3. **Dutch partner submits application**
   - To IND in Netherlands
   - Include all proofs of relationship
   - Pay fees (~â‚¬192 + VR fee)

4. **Wait for IND decision**
   - 90 days official, often longer
   - Can take 3-6 months realistically
   - "Ingebrekestelling" possible after deadline

5. **Collect MVV in Skopje**
   - Travel to North Macedonia
   - Bring all original documents
   - Passport kept for 10 working days

6. **Travel to Netherlands**
   - Within MVV validity period (usually 90 days)
   - Register at municipality within 5 days

7. **Collect Residence Permit**
   - Visit IND desk
   - Biometrics taken
   - Residence card issued

8. **Begin Civic Integration (Inburgering)**
   - 3 years to complete
   - Dutch language to B1 level
   - Knowledge of Dutch society exam
