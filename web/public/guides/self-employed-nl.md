# Self-Employed / Freelancer → Netherlands Guide

## Overview
Work as freelancer or run your own business in the Netherlands.

## Requirements (Points System)
Must score 90+ points across:
- Personal experience (education, business experience)
- Business plan (innovation, market research)
- Added value to NL economy

Source: https://ind.nl/en/residence-permits/work/self-employed

## Key Criteria
- Sufficient income potential (€1,800/month minimum)
- Registered with Chamber of Commerce (KVK)
- Business plan in Dutch
- Proof of contracts/clients

## Timeline
1. **Write business plan** - Must show added value to NL
2. **Get business plan assessed** - Optional but recommended
3. **Apply to IND** - Source: https://ind.nl/en/residence-permits/work/self-employed
4. **IND assessment** (90 days)
5. **Receive MVV** at consulate
6. **Travel to NL**
7. **Register at KVK** - Source: https://www.kvk.nl/english
8. **Register at gemeente** - Source: https://www.government.nl/topics/personal-data/registering-with-your-municipality
9. **Get VAT number** from Belastingdienst - Source: https://www.belastingdienst.nl

## Costs
- Application fee: €1,446
- KVK registration: €75
- Business bank account: €10-30/month

## DAFT Treaty (US/Japan citizens)
Americans and Japanese have easier requirements under DAFT treaty.
Source: https://ind.nl/en/residence-permits/work/self-employed-us-japan
